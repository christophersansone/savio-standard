# Code of Conduct

Yes, a project about the Golden Rule has a code of conduct. It is the Golden Rule, written down — because this project teaches that a standard you won't write down and hold yourself to is a goal, not a standard.

## The standard

Treat everyone in this project's spaces — repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, events — the way you would want yourself and your children treated:

- **Assume goodwill.** Read contributions and critiques in their best light. Most disagreements here will be between people who want the same thing for their kids.
- **Be direct and charitable, in that order together.** Disagree openly, with the person, about the work. No sarcasm at anyone's expense, no piling on, no recruiting allies against a contributor.
- **No gossip.** Do not discuss specific families, children, schools, or administrators in any project space. This is an absolute rule; maintainers will remove such content on sight, including in edit histories.
- **Include the outsider.** Welcome first-time contributors the way a Homeroom Host welcomes a new family: every question gets a kind answer.
- **Repair harm.** If you cause offense, apologize plainly and fix it. If you receive an apology, accept it with grace. We practice what we teach children: genuine repair, then move on.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory remarks, sexualized language or imagery, publishing others' private information, naming or discussing identifiable children, and sustained disruption of discussion. Content contrary to the project's Catholic identity may be declined without it being a judgment of the contributor.

## Scope and enforcement

This code applies in all project spaces and when representing the project publicly. The maintainers enforce this code — with a private word first in most cases, and removal of content or contributors where necessary. Report concerns privately to any maintainer (contact in [README.md](README.md)); reports are handled discreetly. Concerns about a maintainer go to the steward.

Nothing here overrides the safeguarding rule that governs the whole project: anything touching abuse, threats, or crime goes immediately to the appropriate authorities, not to a repository discussion.
