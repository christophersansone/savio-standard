# Contributing

Thank you for helping. This repository is the canonical source for all Savio Project documents and content; formatted documents, the website, and app content are generated from it.

**New to GitHub or Markdown?** Start with [Working With This Repository](docs/working-with-this-repo.md) — it explains why the project works this way and walks through submitting a change from your browser, no tools required.

## How it works

Two words appear throughout this project, and they are not the same thing:

- **The steward** is the *organization* — the entity that holds the project's intellectual property under the [Stewardship Commitment](docs/stewardship-commitment.md) and is accountable for what the project publishes.
- **The maintainers** are *people* — the individuals (at least two, always) who maintain this repository day to day: organizing contributions, keeping collaboration healthy, and giving final approval to changes.

The workflow:

- **Anyone may open issues and pull requests.** Propose edits, corrections, new content, or translations by PR.
- **Maintainers review and merge.** Merged means published-quality; unmerged PRs remain open for discussion. See [docs/why-a-steward.md](docs/why-a-steward.md) for how stewardship and maintainership fit together.
- **Git history is the version log.** Documents carry no version numbers.

## What contributions need the CLA

- **Requires [CLA](CLA.md):** authored content — new documents, substantial rewrites, new sections, meeting kits, skits, playbook material, translations of project content. Agree once by adding this line to your first PR description: `I have read and agree to the Contributor License Agreement (CLA.md).`
- **No CLA needed:** issues, discussion, feedback, typo and formatting fixes, bug reports on generated outputs.

The CLA exists so the whole work stays licensable as one thing — transferable to a future nonprofit, publishable, and commercially licensable to fund the mission. You keep permanent credit and free use of everything. The reasoning is spelled out plainly in [docs/why-a-steward.md](docs/why-a-steward.md).

## Style

- Plain, warm, direct language. Write for a busy parent, not a lawyer or an academic.
- Markdown only; no HTML unless a generator requires it.
- One sentence per idea; short sections; bold lead-ins for list items ("**The point.** Explanation…").
- Catholic identity is native, not decorative: cite Scripture and the Catechism where they genuinely ground a point.
- Never include real names of children, families, schools, or administrators — including in examples, which must be clearly fictional.

## Translations

Translations are among the most valuable contributions. Open an issue first to claim a language, then translate whole documents (not fragments) so each language stays coherent. Translations are content contributions and require the CLA.

## Conduct

All participation is governed by the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) — which is, fittingly, the Golden Rule.
