# The Stewardship Commitment

*The Savio Standard (working title) · Patrons: St. Dominic Savio & St. John Bosco · Working draft*

This Project belongs to the families who live it. But someone must hold the pen, keep the website running, and answer for the whole — a steward: **Savio Standard**, serving until a dedicated entity is formed, acting solely in that capacity — separate from its owner's company and products — and making these commitments publicly, binding itself and any successor:

1. **Free for families, forever.** The Project's own assets — the core content (covenant, glossary, playbooks, formation materials) and the Project website that delivers it — are free to every family and every school. Participation will never carry a price.

2. **Open to every family.** There is no membership and no gate. Every gathering is open to the whole homeroom, grade, or school; invitations always go to all.

3. **Your community is yours.** Every Savio Community is independent. The steward publishes the framework; it will never direct, approve, or control a local community, and it assumes no responsibility or liability for any community's activities — each is accountable for its own.

4. **Credit where credit is due.** Everyone who contributes content is credited by name, permanently.

5. **A clear license, never changed retroactively.** The content is released free for mission use under a public license. Commercial use requires a license from the steward, on published terms. Terms for existing users may loosen over time; they will never retroactively tighten.

6. **Transparency about money.** The mission itself — the content and the website — is sustained by sponsorships and institutional licenses, never by charging families to participate.

7. **Aligned products, clearly optional.** Apps and other products that build on the framework — from the steward's owners or from others under license — are not Project assets and may be paid; none will ever be required to participate. Tools are recommended by published criteria, and anything built by the steward's owners is disclosed as such.

8. **Generosity first.** This work is offered in generosity, by volunteers, for the building of the Kingdom. Because sustaining its core contributors sustains the Project, they may be supported from the sources above: openly, modestly, and never at families' expense.

9. **A worthy successor.** When scale warrants, the mission assets move to a dedicated nonprofit that inherits every commitment on this page.

10. **A graceful ending, if it comes.** If the Project is ever wound down, the core content will be released under an open license so that any community may carry it forward.

11. **Accountability.** Each year the steward publishes a short letter to the community: what was built, what was earned, and what comes next.
